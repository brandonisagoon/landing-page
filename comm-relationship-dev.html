The Johari Window model is a framework designed to help us understand our interpersonal dynamics and perceptions. It has four quadrants: open, hidden, blind, and unknown.

Open

Information we are aware of about ourselves and that others also know.

Hidden

What we are aware of but choose not to reveal to others.

Blind

Aspects others recognize in us, but we are unaware of.

Unknown

Things that remain undiscovered by both ourselves and those around us.

In this analysis, we’ll look at three of my relationships:

▢ My sister Amanda

▢ My friend Matt

▢ My new friend Izzy

Amanda

My Sister

My relationship with Amanda started with mostly “unknowns“. We were both children who didn’t know much about ourselves. We only knew about our roles in the family as the kids. Over time, our relationship became more “open“ — though not in a healthy way. There were many parts of ourselves that we would’ve liked to keep ”hidden”. However, we would often fight and scream about each other’s insecurities in public.

Though our dynamic was quite cruel and unhealthy, we eventually became good friends. Our directness helped us see our “blind“ spots from a place of mutual respect. This has even extended to our relationships with others. We’re both very good at actively listening to feedback and checking how we perceive it.

“Engaging in perception checking and soliciting feedback from others can help us learn more about our blind area” (Weber State University Department of Communication, 2023, section 3.4).

We may both listen to people’s feedback. However, that doesn’t mean we’ll take it.

Matt

My Friend

Matt’s Wedding

Matt and I met at a coffee shop in 2019. At the time, I thought of him as just some dude with a guido accent, wearing a funny-looking fedora — he’s since switched to the occasional beret. I still think he can’t dress himself. It’s a persistent “blind” spot. Back then, I kept those thoughts “hidden” from him. Along with the fact I thought he was an idiot. Eventually, I came to learn that he’s actually not an idiot.

Given that Matt is 6 years older than me, there are lots of “blind” spots and “unknowns” he’s opened me up to. Initially, his girlfriend (now wife) didn’t know they were hanging out with a teenager. Over time, my, shall we say… youthful attributes started to appear. He pointed these behaviors out to me and I am no longer “blind“ to my social awkwardness. It’s just an “open“ fact that I accept.

Matt also introduced me to jazz. I didn’t know that I would have such an affinity for the genre. It was an “unknown” for both of us. I’m not sure I would’ve started going to jazz clubs in New York and getting as deep into the genre as I did without him.

“To discover the unknown, we have to get out of our comfort zones and try new things. We have to pay attention to the things that excite or scare us and investigate them more to see if we can learn something new about ourselves.” (Weber State University Department of Communication, 2023, section 3.4).

Izzy

“Izzert“ My New Friend

Izzy is my new friend, who happens to be my best friend’s girlfriend. We’ve become very good friends very quickly. Much was, and likely still is, “hidden“ from me. One thing that was certainly “hidden” was that she was thinking about making her relationship with my friend official for weeks. I had even inquired about the idea directly. But the time, she just wasn’t ready. Now, it’s out in the “open“. She also makes intensely direct eye contact when speaking. Prior to a conversation about this, she was “blind“ to the matter…

There were also some “blind“ and “hidden“ aspects on my end. She initially perceived me as absurdly high energy and care free. While I’m generally aware of these characteristics, the extent to which they’re seen as abnormal is quite different in Utah compared to the more cosmopolitan environments I’m used to.

Izzy and my best friend are also Bible study leaders. Many of my early interactions with Izzy were within their group of Christian friends.. I am not religious. Though it wasn’t a secret, being the token atheist was a generally “hidden“ fact as I did not volunteer that information unless asked. I wanted to avoid being proselytized and offending people. Over time, my lack of religion has become an “open“ fact and is accepted amongst the group.

Izzy is a new friend to me. There are many “blind”, “hidden”, and “unknown” spots I hope to learn about as our relationship continues to become more “open“. As we’ve both acted quite favorably to our disclosures, I imagine our bond will continue to blossom.

“If the person reacts favorably to our disclosures and reciprocates disclosure, then the cycle of disclosure continues and a deeper relationship may be forged.” (Weber State University Department of Communication, 2023, section 3.4).

Conclusion

The Johari Window has given me a new way of reflecting on my relationships. By applying this framework, I hope to continue to improve the communication within my existing and new relationships.
